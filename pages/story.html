Our Story

The journey of Papa Remo Beach from dream to reality

The Beginning

Papa Remo Beach was born from a love story between Italy and Kenya. In the early 2000s, our founder first visited Watamu and was captivated by its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and the warm hospitality of the local community.

Inspired by the natural beauty of the area and driven by a passion for Italian cuisine and hospitality, the vision for Papa Remo Beach began to take shape. The goal was to create a place that would blend Italian charm with Kenyan warmth, offering visitors an authentic experience that celebrates both cultures.

After years of planning and collaboration with local artisans and community members, Papa Remo Beach opened its doors, quickly becoming a beloved destination for both locals and international visitors.

Our Journey

2005

First Visit to Watamu

Our founder falls in love with the beaches of Watamu and begins dreaming of creating a special place that combines Italian and Kenyan cultures.

2010

Land Acquisition

The perfect beachfront location is found and acquired, with plans drawn up for what would become Papa Remo Beach.

2012

Construction Begins

Building starts on the restaurant and initial beach facilities, with a focus on using local materials and craftspeople.

2014

Grand Opening

Papa Remo Beach officially opens its doors, welcoming guests to experience its unique blend of Italian cuisine and Kenyan hospitality.

2018

Village Development

Construction begins on Papa Remo Village, offering luxury accommodations for those who want to extend their stay.

2022

Expansion

The restaurant is expanded and renovated, and wedding services are introduced to meet growing demand.

2025

Today

Papa Remo Beach continues to grow and evolve while staying true to its original vision of creating a special place where Italian and Kenyan cultures meet.

Community Involvement

At Papa Remo Beach, we believe in giving back to the community that has welcomed us so warmly. We are actively involved in various initiatives that support the local Watamu community:

  • Employment opportunities for local residents
  • Training programs for hospitality skills development
  • Support for local schools and educational programs
  • Beach clean-up initiatives and marine conservation efforts
  • Sourcing ingredients from local farmers and fishermen

We are proud to be part of the Watamu community and are committed to contributing to its sustainable development and prosperity.
